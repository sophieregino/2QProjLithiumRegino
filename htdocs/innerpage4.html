Human Rights

Human rights are rights that all people have, regardless of their race, gender, nationality, ethnicity, language, religion, or other status. The right to life and liberty, freedom from slavery and torture, freedom of thought and speech, the right to employment and education, and many others are all protected by human rights. These rights are available to everyone, without exception.












Racial Justice

Racial justice is the fair treatment of persons of all races in a systematic way that results in equal opportunities and outcomes for all. Racial justice — or racial equity — is more than "anti-racism." It involves the presence of deliberate structures and supports to achieve and sustain racial equity through proactive and preventative actions, as well as the absence of discrimination and disparities.

Diversity

The recognition and celebration of racial diversity is known as racial diversity. Diversity acknowledges and values differences within and between racial identities, recognizing the intersectionality of many groups such as ethnicity, gender, age, religion, disability, sexual orientation, language, and physical appearance.  It also involves different ideas, perspectives, and values

Equality

Equality is the state or quality of being equal; correspindence in quantitiy, degree, value, rank, or ability. This ensures that individuals are not being treated differently or less favorably on the basis of thier specific characteristics.












Inclusion

Inclusion is regarded as a basic human right. The goal of inclusion is to include everyone, regardless of color, gender, disability, medical condition, or other need. It's all about equal access and opportunities, as well as eliminating barriers, discrimination and intolerance.
